Context

Overview

The Los Angeles Public Library (LAPL) is one of the largest public library systems in the United States, consisting of the third largest central library in the country and a network of 72 branches. The Central Library includes nine subject departments that provide reference services to patrons through in-person assistance, telephone, mail, email, and chat. Within this system, librarians play an important role in ensuring that collections, services, and programs meet the needs of Los Angeles’s diverse and multilingual communities.

A Librarian III at LAPL is a mid- to senior-level professional librarian who combines public service with leadership responsibilities. Librarian IIIs may supervise staff, manage branch operations, and coordinate public service desks to ensure that circulation and reference services run smoothly. They also analyze community demographics and adapt library services to meet local needs. Outreach and community engagement are key parts of the position, as librarians often collaborate with schools, community organizations, and local government offices while promoting literacy, digital access, and lifelong learning.

Patty Valdovinos is a Librarian III in Multilingual Collections, focusing on expanding multilingual access to library resources, particularly Spanish-language materials. Her role includes outreach, content creation, translation coordination, and collection development. She helps produce blog posts, social media content, and informational flyers both in person and in-house, ensuring that library information reaches as many patrons as possible. Her department also coordinates translation services and purchases databases and materials in multiple languages. Currently, LAPL acquires resources in nine languages, including Spanish, Korean, Chinese, Farsi, Russian, and Armenian, reflecting the linguistic diversity of Los Angeles.

A significant part of Patty’s responsibilities involves evaluating and selecting Spanish-language materials for the library’s collections. Vendors regularly send title lists for review, and many Spanish-language publishing companies are based internationally. Patty assesses these titles to determine which will best serve library patrons and maintain a relevant and balanced collection. She also consults commercial book lists from companies such as Barnes & Noble to identify additional titles that may benefit the library’s readers. In addition, she works with local independent vendors, including Casita Bookstore and LA Libreria, to purchase materials that support the community’s diverse readership.

The department takes a proactive approach to language access across the library system. Staff work to ensure that important information is translated and distributed so that more patrons can use library services. They also assist other branches in selecting and purchasing Spanish-language materials so these resources are widely available throughout the network. Additionally, the department analyzes circulation statistics for Spanish-language collections to identify gaps, evaluate demand, and determine where improvements can be made. Through outreach, translation, and data-informed collection development, Patty and her team help ensure that multilingual resources remain accessible and responsive to the needs of Los Angeles’s diverse communities.

Professional Context

A Librarian III specializing in Multilingual Collections focuses on developing and managing library materials that serve diverse language communities. This role often includes acting as subject specialist, selecting and purchasing books and databases in multiple languages, especially Spanish, and ensuring that collections accurately represent and respectfully amplify multilingual communities. Librarians in this position research and evaluate materials for language quality, cultural representation, and appropriate content, often working with international vendors as well as major and independent bookstores to acquire materials.
In addition to collection development, they frequently train and support other library staff in serving multilingual patrons and using multilingual resources. The role may also involve promoting the collections through blog posts, social media, and outreach, while helping provide services such as translation resources. A strong emphasis is placed on children’s literature and culturally representative materials, and collections may span many languages ( such as 9 or more) to meet the needs of diverse communities.

Job Market Outlook

The main professional association for this specialization is the American Library Association. Within this network, groups such as the Asian Pacific American Librarians Association support librarians serving Asian and Pacific American communities and promote culturally responsive library services. REFORMA is another association which focuses on improving library services for Latine and Spanish-speaking communities by advocating for Spanish-language collections, supporting bilingual and bicultural library professionals, and supporting outreach programs designed to meet the needs of diverse communities. Multilingual librarians may also participate in the International Federation of Library Associations and Institutions (IFLA), particularly through initiatives that focus on services to multicultural populations and linguistic minorities.

These organizations provide valuable opportunities for professional development through conferences where librarians can present research, attend workshops, and build networks with colleagues. Major events include the ALA Annual Conference, the IFLA World Library and Information Congress, and the REFORMA National Conference. At these conferences, librarians often share research and best practices related to multilingual collections, community outreach, language accessibility, and culturally responsive library services.

Journals that publish in this area include the International Journal of Librarianship, the Journal of Academic Librarianship, Library Journal, and the Canadian Journal of Academic Librarianship. They include articles on diversity, equity, multilingual services, and global librarianship. These journals provide a platform for librarians and researchers to share studies, case reports, and theory on how libraries can better serve linguistically and culturally diverse communities.

Job opportunities for multilingual librarians are advertised through professional job boards such as GovernmentJobs and association career sites. One widely used resource is the ALA JobLIST. They can also be found on job boards associated with the Association of College and Research Libraries as well as HigherEdJobs. If you’re a current UCLA MLIS student, Dee Winn’s listserv is also a great resource.
